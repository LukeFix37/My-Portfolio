Exposure Therapy VR Simulations

Immersive VR experiences for anxiety management and therapeutic intervention

Overview

This project involved developing a virtual reality simulation designed to assist in exposure therapy practices specifically with the fear of spiders. The goal was to create realistic, immersive environments where users could safely confront and manage phobias or anxieties in a controlled virtual space.

Throughout the project, our team worked closely with professional electrical engineers to integrate hardware components and implement Arduino-based systems that interfaced with the VR simulation. This collaborative approach allowed us to create a more interactive and responsive therapeutic experience, blending software and hardware in a meaningful way.

As a highlight of our work, Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak visited our campus and spoke directly to our club about the project. He offered encouragement, shared insights from his career in technology innovation, and expressed enthusiasm for the potential impact of our simulations in mental health treatment.

Technologies Used

  • Unreal Engine 5
  • C++
  • VR headset integration (Meta Quest 2 / Oculus Quest)
  • Arduino microcontroller programming
  • Hardware interfacing with electrical engineering collaborators
  • User feedback capture and adaptive difficulty scaling

Key Features

  • Immersive VR environments tailored to specific phobias (e.g., Spiders)
  • Dynamic difficulty adjustment based on user feedback
  • Arduino-integrated physical feedback systems for enhanced immersion
  • Data collection for therapists to track user progress and session performance

What I Learned

Through this project, I gained valuable experience in VR development workflows, optimizing performance for immersive environments, and integrating real-time feedback systems. It also deepened my knowledge of Unreal Engine’s Blueprints and C++ API for VR applications.

Collaborating with electrical engineers taught me how to effectively bridge the gap between hardware and software, particularly in integrating Arduino-based components with VR systems. Additionally, getting to present our work to Steve Wozniak and hearing his feedback was an unforgettable moment that underscored the importance of innovation and cross-disciplinary teamwork.
